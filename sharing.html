The Trap

Over-engineering = Under-asking

Path A — Build First
  • Define data model for financial ratios
  • Build extraction pipeline from 10-K filings
  • Write rules engine for ratio classification
  • Build rating logic (A/B/C/D thresholds)
  • Generate report template
⏱ 2–3 days · Complex to maintain
Path B — Ask First
  • Describe the business output needed
  • Define the rules as plain language instructions
  • Let AI extract, classify, and write the analysis
  • Review output → refine prompt if needed
⏱ 30 seconds · Self-improving
When you over-engineer, it's often because you didn't ask AI first. Asking AI first changes what you build, not whether you build.

Business Problem

The credit team writes a Credit Memorandum for every new buyer — a 10-page analysis covering financials, risk rating, recommendation. Each one takes a senior analyst half a day to research and write.

The team wants to use AI to draft the first version — but the quality must meet investment-grade credit standards. Formulas are specific to Air8 (not textbook finance). Tone varies by rating band (A vs B vs C buyer). And it needs to get the numbers right.

Non-trivial domain: most devs don't know what EBITDA/Interest coverage means

Business Thinking + Thinking Route

Credit Memo Tool — How We Approached It

Thinking Route
1
Start with the output, not the tech
What does a good CM look like? Get a real example from the analyst.
2
Encode the domain knowledge explicitly
Write Air8's exact formulas (EBITDA = EBIT + D&A, not press release), rating rules, FY labeling into a KB. The model follows rules, it doesn't need to "know" finance.
3
Split into two AI calls
Step 1: extract numbers. Step 2: write analysis. Separating prevents timeout, improves each.
4
Trust the analyst to review, not rebuild
AI drafts. Analyst edits what's wrong. Time savings: 80%+.
Key Insight
Quality lives in the Knowledge Base and prompts, not in the model. Even a weaker (cheaper) model can produce investment-grade analysis if the rules are explicit enough.
Further Enhancement Thinking
+
Save AI raw output (v0) before analyst edits
+
Compare v0 vs final → find patterns → update KB
+
Switch to cheaper model when KB is strong enough

The Framework

Business Result First

Use this mental model every time you approach a new problem with AI.

1
What does the business user actually need?
Not what you think they need — what does the output look like in their hands?
2
What would good output look like?
Find a real example. Get the standard. If it's a document, get the template.
3
Ask AI: "Given [context], produce [output] meeting [criteria]"
Encode the domain rules explicitly. Don't rely on the model's general knowledge.
4
Only then: decide if code is needed
Sometimes AI alone is enough. Sometimes a thin wrapper is all you need. Rarely a full system.
Thinking Route

How to Think, Not Just What to Ask

Getting Started
  • "Show me what good output looks like for this"
  • "What information do I need to produce X?"
  • "Explain [domain concept] as if I'm going to write instructions for an AI"
  • "What are the edge cases or common mistakes in [domain]?"
When Something's Wrong
  • "Here's what you produced vs what I expected — what rule was wrong?"
  • "Why did you [specific decision]? Walk me through your reasoning."
  • "If I wanted this output to be better, what's missing from my instructions?"
  • "Here's a good example and a bad example — what's the difference?"
Thinking Further Ahead
  • "If this works, what would the next limitation be?"
  • "What data would make this smarter over time?"
  • "How would this break at scale or in edge cases?"
  • "What's the simplest way to capture user corrections automatically?"
Key Takeaways

What to Remember

🎯

Start with the output, not the technology

Before writing any code or prompt — define what "good" looks like. Get a real example from the business user.

📚

Quality lives in the KB, not the model

Encode domain rules explicitly. A well-prompted cheaper model beats a smart model with vague instructions.

🔄

Every user edit is free training data

Capture v0 vs v1. The difference between what AI produced and what the expert corrected is your roadmap for improvement.

💡

AI bridges the domain gap — trust it for judgment

You don't need to be a credit analyst to produce credit analysis. You need to know what the output should look like.
